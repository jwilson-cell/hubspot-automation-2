## Part A â€” Helper contract confirmed

Pre-fix helper issues caught during verification:
1. **`/shipments?search=<tracking>` returns empty regardless of filter** even though the tracking code is valid and the matching shipment is visible via the parent order's `shipments[]` array. Likely a scope/permission quirk on bearer-token access. **Workaround**: the `/orders?search=` endpoint matches on nested tracking codes too, so we use that for both identifier types.
2. **Tracker can have `status: "unknown"`** on freshly-shipped orders when SSK hasn't yet polled the carrier â€” real data, just no live carrier state yet. The drafter must not surface "unknown" as a status to the customer.
3. **Tracker can expose `signedBy` and `trackingDetails`** once the package is scanned/delivered â€” added to the normalized output.

### Known limits to note

- SSK's `/shipments` top-level list endpoint doesn't work on bearer-token scope; helper routes everything through `/orders?search=` (works fine). Not a blocker.
- `tracker.status` on freshly-shipped orders is often `"unknown"` until SSK polls the carrier (latency unknown â€” could be minutes or hours). The prompt handles this gracefully.
- `tracking_details` field exists in SSK but was `null` on the sample order. When populated it's a scan history â€” the prompt instructs the drafter to pick one representative scan rather than paste the whole array.
- Real customer reply verification (the "reply routes back to HubSpot" flow from the path-2 work) is independent of SSK hydration and already verified.
